# Business Requirements and Threat Context

## Why this project was created

This repository was created to demonstrate a practical defense-in-depth architecture in response to the growing impact of modern cyberattacks on organisations of all sizes.

The design was influenced by the reality that multiple cybersecurity breaches have recently affected large enterprises, Fortune 500 companies, trusted suppliers, and federal/public-sector organisations. These events showed that strong brands, large budgets, and existing technology stacks do not remove risk.

## Threat drivers

Key drivers behind this business requirement include:

- Supply chain attacks through software vendors, managed service providers, and trusted third parties
- Ransomware and extortion campaigns
- Credential theft through phishing and business email compromise
- Data exposure caused by weak segmentation or cloud misconfiguration
- Lateral movement after initial compromise
- Delayed detection caused by limited logging and monitoring

## Business requirement

The business requires a security architecture that can:

1. Protect internet-facing and internal systems using layered controls
2. Support SME growth while remaining suitable for larger regulated environments
3. Reduce dependence on any single security control
4. Detect attacks early and improve incident response
5. Protect sensitive business, financial, operational, and customer data
6. Improve operational resilience through backup and recovery design
7. Manage third-party and supplier access risk more effectively

## Project objective

The objective of this repository is to show recruiters and hiring managers that the author can connect real infrastructure and security experience to a structured security architecture and business-aligned control model.
